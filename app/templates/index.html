FFFold

FFFold is a web application for the local optimization of protein structures predicted by the AlphaFold2 algorithm and deposited in the AlphaFold DB database. The structures are optimized by the PPROpt method powered by physics-based force-fields GFN-FF, which results is comparable to the optimization of whole protein structure with constrained α-carbons. Thus, FFFold optimizes in particular the bond lengths and angles and describes the interactions between nearby residues as can be seen in examples. Before computation of the charges, input protein structures is protonated by PROPKA3. The details about the methodology and usage are described in the manual. This website is free and open to all users and there is no login requirement. Source codes are freely available at GitHub.

Examples

Dihedral angles
The AlphaFold 2 and the subsequent PROPKA prediction often created a bond with a unnatural angle (e.g., peptide bond with almost right angle). FFFold optimizes bond lengths and bond angles.
Hydrogen bonds
Hydrogen bonds are important contributors to the secondary and partly also to the tertiary structure of the protein. During FFFold optimization, a large number of hydrogen bonds are formed/refined in the structure.
π–π stacking
The π-π interaction stabilizes the secondary and tertiary structure of the protein. Also these interactions can be described because force-fields GFN-FF retains an iterative Hückel quantum-mechanical scheme for aromatic atoms.
